Scaling Operations at Branch

Systems Observations + Strategic Readiness

⏱ Estimated reading time: 2 minutes

1. Observations from Launch Work (Dec 2024 – Mar 2025)

  • Factory-to-field coordination leaned on Slack threads; handoffs lacked structure
  • High workflow variation led to inconsistent quality and rework
  • Metrics were tracked reactively; limited foresight into delays
  • Critical process knowledge was not codified into SOPs
  • Biggest constraint wasn't complexity, it was process entropy

2. Systems I've Built to Solve These Problems

  • Automated KPI dashboards tied to comp and updated from CRM activity
  • Built cadence compliance and task enforcement layers to ensure throughput
  • Implemented validation logic and deduplication workflows for clean data
  • Condensed multi-hour reporting into logic-driven summaries in Sheets
  • Enforced cross-role accountability with task systems and daily review loops

3. Fit for Director of Operations

This role mirrors the conversation we had earlier this year. I've led operational transformations where waste, not tooling, was the core constraint. My background includes:

  • Designing systems to scale throughput and reduce variability
  • Enforcing accountability and visibility through process and metrics
  • Contributing directly to operational maturity in lean orgs

Having worked inside Branch as a contractor, I understand the culture, constraints, and mission. I'm ready to drive this next phase of scale with clarity and ownership.
